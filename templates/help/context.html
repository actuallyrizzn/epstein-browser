Official Context & Sources

Official House Oversight Committee releases and document sources

Official Release: The documents in this browser were released by the House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform following a congressional subpoena to the U.S. Department of Justice.

Congressional Action

Initial Subpoena (August 5, 2025)

House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform Chairman James Comer (R-Ky.) issued deposition subpoenas to:

  • Bill and Hillary Clinton - Former President and Secretary of State
  • Former U.S. Attorneys General: James Comey, Loretta Lynch, Eric Holder, Merrick Garland, Robert Mueller, William Barr, Jeff Sessions, and Alberto Gonzales
  • Former FBI Directors: James Comey and Robert Mueller

The Committee also issued a subpoena to the U.S. Department of Justice for records related to Jeffrey Epstein.

Deposition Schedule:
• Department of Justice Records: August 19
• Former U.S. Attorney General William Barr: August 18
• Former U.S. Attorney General Alberto Gonzales: August 26
• Former U.S. Attorney General Jeff Sessions: August 28
• Former FBI Director Robert Mueller: September 2
• Former U.S. Attorney General Loretta Lynch: September 9
• Former U.S. Attorney General Eric Holder: September 30
• Former U.S. Attorney General Merrick Garland: October 2
• Former FBI Director James Comey: October 7
• Former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton: October 9
• Former President Bill Clinton: October 14

DOJ Compliance (August 18, 2025)

Chairman Comer confirmed that the Department of Justice informed the Committee it would begin providing Epstein-related records, with the Trump Administration's commitment to transparency.

Records Release (September 2, 2025)

The House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform released 33,295 pages of Epstein-related records provided by the U.S. Department of Justice.

About This Browser

This document browser was created to provide public access to the congressional records released by the House Oversight Committee. It includes:

  • Search functionality - Full-text search across all documents
  • OCR processing - Optical character recognition for scanned documents
  • Local processing - Works with downloaded document files
  • Open source - Transparent, auditable codebase
Transparency: This browser is designed to make congressional records accessible to the public while maintaining appropriate privacy protections for victims.
